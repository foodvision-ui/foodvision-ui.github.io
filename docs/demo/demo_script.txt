FoodVision AI — 3-Minute Demo Script
Target runtime: 180s. Roughly 440 words at a calm pace.
Cues in [brackets] tell the speaker what's on screen.


[SCENE 1 — 00:00 to 00:25, home page + login]

Hi, this is FoodVision AI. The pitch is simple. Take a photo of what's in
your fridge, tell it what you feel like eating, and it hands you back a
recipe the model thinks you'll actually make.

[00:05 — click Sign Up / Login]

I'll sign in with the demo account. Email, password, submit.

[00:18 — modal closes, topbar flips to Logout]

I'm in. The topbar shows the account chip. Over to the planner.


[SCENE 2 — 00:25 to 01:25, first meal, AI learns]

[00:25 — planner page, empty history]

Fresh account, no meal history yet.

[00:28 — drop fridge.jpg on upload zone]

I drop in a fridge photo. Tomatoes, pasta, eggs, a block of cheese.

[00:33 — typing in message box]

In the message box I type, "i like italian food, what can i make tonight."
Short and casual, the way anyone would ask.

[00:40 — click Generate, image preview appears, skeleton loads]

Hit generate. The backend is running Qwen3-VL-32B. It looks at the image,
reads my message, and plans a meal. While it's thinking, here's the bit
worth explaining. The request carries a user profile object. On this
account it starts empty. Qwen is about to infer that I like Italian and
savory food from what I just typed, and the backend writes those
inferences back onto my Jac graph as AI-learned fields.

[01:10 — recipe card renders, "Profile updated" chip]

Recipe's back. Dish, reason, ingredients, steps, macros. See that chip at
the top, "Profile updated." Cuisine got Italian. Flavors got savory.

[01:20 — click Profile in nav]

Let me jump to the profile page.


[SCENE 3 — 01:25 to 02:20, manual override]

[01:25 — profile page, green cards with "AI learned" pill]

The cuisine and flavor cards are outlined in green with an AI learned
pill. That's how I know the model put them there, not me.

[01:30 — click Edit preferences]

Now I want to correct it. I actually cook Japanese and Korean most nights.

[01:38 — edit cuisine field]

Cuisine field goes from Italian to Japanese, Korean.

[01:44 — type into allergies]

I'll add peanuts to allergies while I'm here. That's my own input, not
inferred.

[01:50 — change skill dropdown]

And I bump cooking skill from Intermediate to Advanced.

[01:55 — click Save]

Save.

[02:00 — cards re-render in orange]

Cards re-render. Cuisine is orange now. The AI learned pill is gone
because these are my edits. Allergies shows peanuts. Skill reads advanced.

[02:10 — click Planner]

Back to the planner.


[SCENE 4 — 02:20 to 03:00, second meal uses edited profile]

[02:20 — drop leftovers.jpg]

Second photo. Leftover rice, soy sauce, scallions.

[02:24 — type message]

Message reads, "something quick for dinner." No cuisine hint this time. I
want the profile to do the talking.

[02:30 — click Generate]

[02:32 — open DevTools, Network tab, analyze_meal Payload]

While it's loading, I'll crack open devtools. Here's the analyze_meal
payload. The user profile carries Japanese and Korean cuisine, peanuts in
allergies, skill set to advanced. The edits I made on the profile page
are flowing straight into the Qwen prompt.

[02:45 — result renders]

Result lands. Dish is Japanese. The recipe steers around peanuts on its
own. No rice-and-soy-sauce Korean suggestion the image alone would have
pushed for.

[02:52 — click Delivery]

Quick look at the delivery page. Local restaurants from OpenStreetMap if I
don't feel like cooking.

[02:58 — cut to home, outro card]

That's FoodVision AI. Jac backend, Qwen3-VL-32B, and a profile that
listens to both the model and the user.
